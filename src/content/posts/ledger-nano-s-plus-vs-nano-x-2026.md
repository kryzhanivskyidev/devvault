## Ledger Nano S Plus — $79

The Nano S Plus is Ledger's entry-level device for serious users. It replaced the original Nano S in 2022 with a bigger screen, USB-C, and significantly more storage.

**Who it's for:**
- Users who manage crypto from a desktop computer
- Those who don't need mobile access
- Budget-conscious buyers who want professional security without the premium price

**Strengths:**
- Supports 5,500+ cryptocurrencies
- CC EAL5+ Secure Element chip
- Full Ledger Live support
- Compact and durable

**Limitations:**
- No Bluetooth — USB only
- Can't use with Ledger Live Mobile
- Fewer simultaneous apps installed

## Ledger Nano X — $149

The Nano X adds Bluetooth connectivity and a battery, making it the go-to choice for mobile users and frequent travelers.

**Who it's for:**
- Users who manage crypto on their phone
- Travelers who need wallet access on the go
- Power users who hold many different coins simultaneously

**Strengths:**
- Works with Ledger Live Mobile (iOS and Android)
- Bluetooth for wireless connection
- Battery lasts up to 8 hours
- More storage for simultaneous apps

**Limitations:**
- $70 more expensive than Nano S Plus
- Bluetooth adds a (very small) attack surface
- Bulkier than Nano S Plus

## Security — Are They Equal?

**Yes.** Both devices use the same **CC EAL5+ certified Secure Element chip** and the same **BOLOS operating system**. Your private keys never leave the hardware on either model.

The security architecture is identical. The Nano X's Bluetooth implementation is isolated from the Secure Element — so even if Bluetooth were compromised, it couldn't access your keys.

## Which One Should You Buy?

**Choose Nano S Plus if:**
- You use crypto mainly at home or at a desk
- You don't need mobile access
- You want to save $70 without sacrificing security

**Choose Nano X if:**
- You manage crypto from your phone
- You travel frequently and want wireless access
- You hold 5+ different cryptocurrencies simultaneously

## What About Ledger Stax and Flex?

Ledger also makes the **Stax ($279)** and **Flex ($249)** — both feature touchscreens and premium builds. They're excellent devices, but for most users the Nano X covers everything at a lower price.

The Stax and Flex make sense if you actively use NFTs or want the most modern interface experience.

## Bottom Line

For **most users**, the **Nano S Plus at $79** is the right call — same security, lower price, perfectly functional for desktop use. If mobile access matters to you, the **Nano X at $149** is worth every penny of the difference.
