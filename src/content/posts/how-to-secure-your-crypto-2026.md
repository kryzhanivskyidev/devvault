Crypto security in 2026 is non-negotiable. With billions lost annually to hacks, phishing, and human error, protecting your assets requires more than a strong password. This guide covers everything you need — from hardware wallets to seed phrase storage to phishing defense.

## Why Software Wallets Aren't Enough

A software wallet (MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Exodus) stores your private keys on a device connected to the internet. That means:

- Malware can extract your keys while you sleep
- A compromised browser extension can drain your wallet in seconds
- A phishing site can trick you into signing a malicious transaction

**Hardware wallets solve this** by keeping your private keys on a dedicated offline chip that never connects to the internet. Transactions must be physically approved on the device — no malware can fake that button press.

## Layer 2 — Seed Phrase Security

Your 24-word recovery phrase is the master key to your entire wallet. If someone gets it, they own your crypto — permanently, with no recourse.

### What NOT to Do

- ❌ Photo of recovery phrase on your phone
- ❌ Typed into any app, email, or document
- ❌ Stored in Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox
- ❌ Shared with anyone — ever, for any reason
- ❌ Entered on any website (this is always a scam)

### What TO Do

- ✅ Write on paper using the card included with your device
- ✅ Store in a fireproof safe or safety deposit box
- ✅ Consider a **metal backup** (steel plates) for fire/flood resistance
- ✅ Make two copies stored in different physical locations

### Metal Backup Options

Paper degrades. A house fire destroys it in seconds. Metal seed phrase plates are stamped or engraved with your words and survive fires up to 1400°C and floods indefinitely. Ledger sells compatible accessories in their official store.

## Layer 3 — Device Security

Your hardware wallet is only as safe as the environment it operates in. Your computer or phone can still be attacked.

**OS and software hygiene:**
- Keep your OS updated — most exploits target known vulnerabilities
- Use a dedicated browser profile or browser for crypto activities
- Install only necessary extensions — each one is an attack surface
- Enable full-disk encryption (FileVault on Mac, BitLocker on Windows)

**For Mac users**, Apple's **FileVault** encrypts your entire drive. If your laptop is stolen, your files are unreadable without your login password.

## Layer 4 — Account Security

Every exchange and web3 account needs strong, unique credentials.

**Password manager:** Use a dedicated password manager (not your browser's built-in one). Generate a unique 20+ character password for every account. Never reuse passwords.

**2FA everywhere:**
- Use an authenticator app (not SMS — SIM swapping is a real attack vector)
- Authy or Google Authenticator for TOTP codes
- Hardware security keys (YubiKey) for maximum protection on exchanges

**Exchange accounts:**
- Whitelist withdrawal addresses where available
- Enable login notifications
- Use a dedicated email address for crypto — not your personal or work email

## Layer 5 — Phishing Defense

Phishing is responsible for more crypto losses than technical hacks. Attackers create near-perfect fake versions of MetaMask, Ledger Live, Coinbase, and every major exchange.

**Rules that never break:**
- Always type exchange URLs manually or use saved bookmarks
- Never click links in emails claiming to be from crypto services
- Verify the URL carefully — `ledger.com` vs `ledger-app.com` vs `1edger.com`
- Ledger will **never** ask for your 24-word recovery phrase — not in any app, email, or support chat

**Browser protection:**
- Use uBlock Origin to block malicious ads
- MetaMask has built-in phishing detection — keep it updated
- Consider a DNS-level blocker (NextDNS, Pi-hole) for your home network

## Layer 6 — Network Security

**Use a VPN** on public Wi-Fi. Coffee shops, airports, and hotels are prime hunting grounds for man-in-the-middle attacks. A VPN encrypts all your traffic and hides your real IP.

This matters especially when:
- Checking exchange balances on public networks
- Approving transactions while traveling
- Running a crypto node
